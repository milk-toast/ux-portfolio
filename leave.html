Opportunity

Following the MVP launch of MyBenefits, The Hartford was looking to integrate the leave of absence management feature set into the new site. Leave is a very complicated process, and we were looking to find ways to translate that process into something simple and intuitive for our users.

Research

Current State

In order to know how to design for the future, we needed to ground ourselves on the current state, and why it was so confusing to our users

Absences

When am I approved to be out of work?The current system shows a table with all requests for time off. It's up to the user to make sense of it, and understand when they're approved to be out.

Balance

How much time do I have left for future absences?The current system shows balances remaining for different leave plans, but there is no concept of an overall balance

Research

Interviews & Synthesis

We conducted a couple of rounds of research with existing users to identify key points to focus on with our design. After synthesizing our findings, we determined three priorities for the project

1

Show me my leave in terms I can understand

2

Tell me when I am approved to take absences

3

Tell me how much time balance I have for future absences

Design

Identifying Leaves of Absence

We needed a clear design for the leave of absence tiles that display on the site's landing page. Our research showed that users identify their leaves based on when they will be out, and the reason they are going out of work.

We tweaked the design of the claim tiles from the previous MyBenefits release to accomplish this goal. We also came up with a way to group disability claims with leaves when they are related.

Design

Consolidating Absences

We wanted to reduce the cognitive load of figuring out when someone is approved to miss work. To do this, we proposed designs that would consolidate future absence requests, thereby eliminating a lot of the mental calculations our users were making with the previous state.

Design

Overall Balance

Leaves of absence currently draw time from a multitude of plans based on federal, state and municipal laws, as well as an employer's own policies. This is confusing. We proposed the concept of an overall balance that worked out all the math behind the scenes.
