OPPORTUNITY

Public Art can be a vital part of any neighborhood. It exhibits the character of that neighborhood, and is a reflection of those who live there. The current process of painting murals flows solely through business owners and artists. Community members have no say as to what are they will see on the walls of their neighborhood on a daily basis.

For this project, our team set out to solve the issue of community involvement in public art through our mobile app, 14FT.

THE PROCESS

CURRENT

BUSINESS OWNER

Reaches out to artist for a commissioned work

ARTIST

Confirms their interest and paints the mural

PROPOSED

BUSINESS OWNER

Lists available wall on 14FT

ARTIST

Applies for available wall by submitting a proposed mural on 14FT

COMMUNITY

Vote on proposed murals on 14FT

LOGO

Our logo is meant to resemble the corner view of a building. The red is meant to represent a brick wall, while the blue represents a pale sky.

FINAL PRODUCT

HOMEPAGE

The homepage focuses on walls a user has interacted with in a section called “My Walls,” as well as walls that the user can vote oncalled “Walls in Your Area.”

PROFILE PAGE

Users can go to their profile to edit their basic information and to see what is going on with walls they have voted on.

OPEN WALLS PAGE

Users can vote on submissions for walls in their areaby going to an open wall’s page. There they can see the different artist submittions and vote for the artist of their choice.

ARTIST SUBMISSION PAGE

If a user is interested in learning more about a submission, or about the artists themselves, they can go to a dedicated page for each submission to get more details.

CLOSING THOUGHTS

I loved working on this project. Beyond getting to work with a subject matter in street art that is near and dear to me, it was a great experience in applying a lot of the interaction design and information architecture lessons I had learned at General Assembly, as well as exploring new grounds with the visual design process.

I was very pleased with our final product, and believe that we accomplished the user goals we created at the outset of the project; we made an intuitive and visually appealing mobile app that allowed business owners, artists and community members to collaborate on public art in a meaningful way.
