Opportunity

Public Art can be a vital part of any neighborhood. It exhibits the character of that neighborhood, and can be a reflection of those who live there. At the moment, community member have no say on what art goes up in their neighborhood.

For this personal project, our team set out to solve the issue of community involvement in public art through our mobile app, 14FT.

Personas

Based on 14 total interviews with business owners, artists, and community members, we were able to create four persons.

Sandy Store Owner

I want to support my neighborhood culture and work with artists that understand our community.

Aaron Street Artist

I want more people to see my art so I can grow in my craft.

Lilly Community Member, Mother

I want family-friendly murals so I can expose my kids to art in a responsible way.

Sasha Community Member, Single

I want to maintain and promote the culture of my neighborhood.

Final Result

I loved working on this project. Beyond getting to work with a subject matter in street art that is near and dear to me, it was a great experience in applying a lot of the interaction design and information architecture lessons I had learned at General Assembly, as well as exploring new grounds with the visual design process. I was very pleased with our final product, and believe that we accomplished the user goals we created at the outset of the project; we made an intuitive and visually appealing mobile app that allowed business owners, artists and community members to collaborate on public art in a meaningful way.
