Modernizing through an atomic design system

Opportunity

As part of a company-wide rebranding effort, The Hartford was looking to build a new, responsive group benefits website by creating a clear and intuitive design system and modernizing its branding and visual design.

Exploration Phase

We relied on three key insights from a leading service design agency in order to guide our work. We determined 8 common use pages that could drive at the key insights while also covering a diverse cross section of design components and patterns. With these pages in mind, we built out medium fidelity wireframes as our design foundation.

Design System

Atomic Design

We began dissecting our 8 common use pages to build out a foundation for our design system based on atomic design principles. Later analysis showed that through just 8 screens we covered roughly 90% of atoms, 50% of molecules and 20% of organisms with just our 10 common use pages.

Visual Design

We took our medium-fidelity components from the 8 common use pages and applied a branded look and feel. From this, we then created our visual language for the system.

Implementation

Sprint Schedule

  • The design team worked very closely with the product and process teams to ensure that we covered all desired functionality while adhering to the underlying claims processes at our insurance client.
  • We also worked closely with the UI and services development teams to ensure that we were delivering designs that were feasible and extensible.

  • In order to meet deliverables within our Agile delivery cycles, we adopted the following schedule where design and product worked in tandem to be "sprint ahead" of development and testing.

Sprint Activities

Within each three week sprint, we completed the following activities to make sure that we were working closely with each team and delivering on time.

Week 1 Planning

Review pages and features with Product and Process leads

Assign work to designers

Review previous designs and identify any potential overlap in functionality or patterns

Begin mobile design

Week 2 Feedback

Validate that mobile designs meet Product expectations

Validate that mobile designs follow Process guidelines

Identify reused patterns with Development team

Validate all data in designs is feasible with Services team

Receive sign off from all groups

(If design is net new) complete a round of usability testing

Week 3 Delivery

Deliver final mobile designs

Add mobile designs into sitewide InVision prototype for consumption by other teams

Create and deliver the tablet and desktop designs

Closing Thoughts

Over time, we reused and extended the design system from its foundation to cover the ~120 pages in the final application. Our design team met all our goals by creating a modern and extensible atomic design system that will be the future building blocks for group benefits applications at our insurance client.
