OPPORTUNITY

Public Art can be a vital part of any neighborhood. It exhibits the character of that neighborhood, and is a reflection of those who live there. The current process of painting murals flows solely through business owners and artists. Community members have no say as to what are they will see on the walls of their neighborhood on a daily basis.

For this project, our team set out to solve the issue of community involvement in public art through our mobile app, 14FT.

CLOSING THOUGHTS

Groove Maps was my first experience working directly with a client on user experience creation. I found it to be a great learning experience for putting my General Assembly lessons into action. While most of the work centered around feature integration, something that was part of our curriculum, we also had some new learning on how to take an existing design system and extend it to new pages.

All in all, the client was thrilled with the amount of work we got done in a short period of time, and loved the data-driven approach we took to validating the new functionality.
